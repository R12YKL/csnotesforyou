Ethics & Computing

Introduction to Computing Ethics

Computing ethics examines the moral principles and values that guide the use of computing technology.

As computers become increasingly integrated into society, ethical considerations in technology development and use are more important than ever.

Core Ethical Principles

Integrity

  • Being honest and transparent
  • Admitting mistakes
  • Following through on commitments
  • In computing: Creating secure, truthful systems

Responsibility

  • Being accountable for decisions and actions
  • Considering consequences of technology
  • Protecting user data and privacy

Privacy

  • Respecting personal information
  • Data collection should be ethical and transparent
  • Users should control their own data

Fairness and Bias

  • Ensuring systems treat all users equitably
  • Avoiding discrimination in algorithms
  • Transparent decision-making processes

Security

  • Protecting against unauthorized access
  • Securing personal and sensitive data
  • Responsible disclosure of security vulnerabilities

Ethical Issues in Computing

Data Privacy

Issue: Companies collecting vast amounts of personal data

Considerations:
• What data should be collected?
• How should user consent be obtained?
• Who has access to the data?
• How long should data be retained?
• What are user rights regarding their data?

Artificial Intelligence and Automation

Issue: AI systems making decisions that affect humans

Considerations:
• Can AI systems be biased?
• Who is responsible if AI makes a wrong decision?
• Should humans always be in the loop?
• What about job displacement?

Cybersecurity and Hacking

Issue: Balancing security with usability

Considerations:
• Is it ethical to create security tools?
• What about ethical hacking to find vulnerabilities?
• Responsibility for data breaches
• How to protect vulnerable users

Intellectual Property

Issue: Protecting software and digital content

Considerations:
• Software piracy and licensing
• Open-source vs proprietary software
• Copyright of digital works
• Fair use and sharing

Digital Divide

Issue: Unequal access to technology

Considerations:
• Some have access to technology; others don't
• Cost barriers to technology
• Internet access varies by region
• Impact on education and opportunity

Environmental Impact

Issue: Computing's effect on the environment

Considerations:
• Energy consumption of data centers
• E-waste from discarded electronics
• Mining for rare earth elements
• Carbon footprint of computing

Military and Defense Applications

Ethical Considerations:

  • Autonomous weapons and their control
  • Surveillance capabilities and privacy invasion
  • Cyberwarfare and attribution
  • Development of systems for harm
Example: Autonomous drones

• Can they be programmed to target correctly?
• Who is responsible for decisions?
• What about civilian casualties?
• Can they be hacked or misdirected?

Self-Driving Cars: An Ethical Case Study

The Moral Dilemma:

A self-driving car detects an unavoidable crash. It can either:
• Swerve left and harm pedestrians
• Swerve right and harm passengers
• Continue straight and harm both

Questions:
• Can a car be programmed to make this choice?
• Is it ethical to program one outcome over another?
• Who is liable: manufacturer, programmer, or owner?
• Should human input override the AI?

Other Self-Driving Car Considerations:

  • Cybersecurity: What if the car is hacked?
  • Surveillance: Constant location tracking
  • Job displacement: Millions of taxi/delivery drivers
  • Accessibility: Benefits for disabled users
  • Reliability: How safe must it be?

Professional Ethics and Codes of Conduct

Computing Professionals' Responsibility:

  • ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) Code of Ethics
  • IEEE Computer Society Code of Ethics
  • Duty to society, employers, and users
  • Continuous learning and professional development

When to Refuse Work:

  • Projects that harm society
  • Systems designed to deceive
  • Work violating privacy or security
  • Projects supporting unethical practices

Legislation and Regulations

Important Laws:

  • GDPR (EU): Strict data privacy protection
  • CCPA (California): Consumer privacy rights
  • HIPAA: Medical data privacy (USA)
  • Copyright Law: Digital content protection
  • Computer Fraud Act: Unauthorized access criminalized

Making Ethical Decisions

Framework for Ethical Decision-Making:

  1. Identify the issue: What is the ethical problem?
  2. Gather information: Who is affected? What are consequences?
  3. Consider alternatives: What options exist?
  4. Evaluate using principles: What is right/fair/just?
  5. Make and justify decision: Choose and explain reasoning
  6. Implement and evaluate: Act and review outcomes

Key Revision Points

  • Computing ethics examines moral principles in technology use
  • Core principles: integrity, responsibility, privacy, fairness, security
  • Major ethical issues: privacy, AI bias, cybersecurity, digital divide
  • Autonomous systems raise complex moral questions
  • Self-driving cars exemplify ethical dilemmas in computing
  • Professional codes guide computing professionals' conduct
  • Regulations protect privacy and data rights
  • Ethical decision-making requires systematic thinking
